Abstract

Scaling test-time compute brings substantial performance gains for large language models (LLMs). By sampling multiple answers and heuristically aggregate their answers (e.g., either through majority voting or using verifiers to rank the answers), one can achieve consistent performance gains while increasing test-time compute in math domains. In this paper, we propose a new way to leverage such multiple sample set. We train a small LLM, called Analyzer, that takes a concatenated sequence of multiple samples and output the final answer, optimizing it for the answer accuracy with reinforcement learning. Experiments on multiple reasoning datasets show that Analyzer outperforms other test-time scaling methods such as reward model-based re-ranking. Our analyzer also shows a promising generalization ability, across sample set sizes, base model family and sizes, and tasks. By separating LLMs to generate answers and LLMs to analyze and aggregate sampled answers, our approach can work with the outputs from premier black box models easily and efficiently.
